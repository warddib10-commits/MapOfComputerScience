A Multilevel Map of Computer Science

The Map of Computer Science (AI & Robotics)

made by Ward Dib

Hello, my name is Ward Dib. I started working on this project on May 20, and the goal was not only to list information about artificial intelligence and robotics, but to organize it as an explorable knowledge system.

This map is based on Denning’s Area 7 — Artificial Intelligence & Robotics. It maps the field across three main layers: theory, models & measurements, and implementations. Each node has a short explanation, its theory / experiment / design activities, important people, open problems, venues, examples, related areas, and connections to UVT.

The major part of this work was extracting the main ideas from Denning’s text, researching the global researchers and venues, connecting every open problem to its subfields, and researching the UVT dimension — linking faculty members, research groups, and related projects into the map.

A quick guide to using the map
  • Use the Core Map to see the main structure of AI & Robotics.
  • Use the Connected Map (the second button on the slider) to reveal the most important relationships between areas.
  • Use the Full Atlas to explore the complete network — people, problems, venues, UVT contributors, and neighbouring CS fields.
  • Use the search bar to find a specific topic, researcher, venue, or problem.

Sources & Methodology

How this atlas was built

This map charts Denning’s Area 7 — Artificial Intelligence & Robotics. Its structure follows the Denning model of the discipline; node content draws on standard reference literature and, for the local dimension, on publicly listed information from the West University of Timișoara (UVT).

Disciplinary structure
  • P. J. Denning et al. — Computing as a Discipline / Computer Science: The Discipline
  • ACM & IEEE-CS — Computer Science Curricula (CS2023) knowledge areas
  • AAAI framing of AI & Robotics as a field
Global field — people & venues
  • Conferences: NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR, AAAI, IJCAI, ACL, ICRA, RSS, CoRL
  • Journals: Artificial Intelligence, JAIR, IEEE TPAMI, JMLR, IJRR
  • Researcher associations based on public academic profiles and the listed works / venues
Local dimension — UVT
  • West University of Timișoara — Faculty of Mathematics & Computer Science (info.uvt.ro)
  • Department of Computer Science — faculty pages & listed research areas
  • Local research groups & infrastructure (AI / data-science groups, HPC)
  • SYNASC — Int. Symposium on Symbolic and Numeric Algorithms for Scientific Computing (synasc.ro)
Methodology note

UVT faculty are mapped to AI & Robotics sub-areas on the basis of their department pages and publicly listed research areas — not private CVs. These are indicative associations for the purpose of the map, not official institutional assignments.
